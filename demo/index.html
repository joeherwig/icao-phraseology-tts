Include it

simply adding the js file in your website like
<!DOCTYPE html> <html> <head> ... <script type="text/javascript" src="https://gitlab.com/joeherwig/joeherwig-webcomponents/-/raw/master/webcomponents/joeherwig-flightboard.js"></script>

in your webpage..

Then use it where you want to add your ICAO content like that:

... <body> ... <joeherwig-atc-phraseology> ... <div class="acft">Stuttgart Delivery, DLH22G, Position 12, Information K, request Startup and clearance to Zurich.</div> <div class="atc">DLH22G, good morning, say again your Position.</div> ... <div class="acft">DLH22G, we're on Position 12.</div> <div class="atc">DLH22G, startup is approved. Cleared to Zurich via Rotweil 5B Departure. Climb Altitude 5000ft.</div> </joeherwig-atc-phraseology> ...

of course you can also structure your aeronautical radio documentation with headlines, paragraphs, lists, figures and images etc.

we just work with divs of classes atc and acft.

Spelling hints

Callsigns

For a couple of airlines we're able to map the callsign to the telephony designator.
Per default we're trying to recognize the callsign by starting with the ICAO three letter airline designator and followed by one till four alphanumeric digits.
As ICAO only offers an paid API I decided to include a list of ICAO Airline designators and mapped them to telephony designators.
